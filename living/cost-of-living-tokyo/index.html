Cost of Living in Tokyo 2025: Complete Monthly Breakdown

January 20, 2026 · 3 min read ·
cost of living tokyo expat finances japan living costs monthly budget

Tokyo has a reputation for being expensive — but is it really? Compared to cities like London, New York, or Sydney, Tokyo is surprisingly affordable once you know where to look. Here’s a real monthly breakdown for expats in 2025.

In USD (at ¥150/$1): Budget ~$800–1,000/month, Comfortable ~$1,300–1,700/month.

Rent in Tokyo

Rent is by far the biggest expense. Prices vary massively by location and size.

Average Rents by Apartment Type (2025)

SizeCentral TokyoMid-ring (30 min)Suburban (50 min)
1K (25 m²)¥80,000–120,000¥60,000–80,000¥45,000–65,000
1DK (35 m²)¥100,000–150,000¥75,000–100,000¥55,000–80,000

Pro tip: Areas like Adachi, Katsushika, Edogawa, and Itabashi offer significantly lower rents while still being within 30–40 minutes of central Tokyo by train.

Food Costs

Food in Tokyo is very affordable compared to Western cities:

  • Convenience store meal (7-Eleven, Lawson): ¥500–¥800
  • Ramen at a local shop: ¥700–¥1,200
  • Set lunch (teishoku) at a restaurant: ¥800–¥1,500
  • Sushi (conveyor belt, kaitenzushi): ¥1,000–¥2,000
  • Supermarket dinner ingredients (for 2): ¥800–¥1,500

If you cook at home regularly, you can eat well on ¥25,000–¥35,000/month per person.

Transport

Tokyo’s public transport is world-class. Most expats don’t need a car.

  • Monthly commuter pass (30-min commute): ¥8,000–¥15,000
  • IC card (Suica/Pasmo) daily use: ¥200–¥500 per trip
  • Bicycle: ¥3,000–¥10,000/month (rental) or one-time purchase
  • Taxi: ¥730 starting fare — expensive for regular use

If you’re considering life outside Tokyo where a car is needed, read our guide on buying property in rural Japan.

Utilities

Monthly utility costs depend on the size of your apartment and season:

UtilityTypical Monthly Cost
Electricity¥4,000–¥10,000
Gas¥2,000–¥5,000
Water¥2,000–¥3,500 (billed every 2 months)
Internet¥3,500–¥5,000 (home fiber)

Summer (AC) and winter (heating) push electricity bills higher — expect ¥8,000–¥12,000 in peak months.

Health Insurance

All residents in Japan (including foreigners) must enroll in health insurance:

  • National Health Insurance (NHI): Based on your income — typically ¥5,000–¥25,000/month. Covers 70% of medical costs.
  • Employer health insurance: If your company enrolls you, premiums are shared. Usually better coverage.

Doctor visits with NHI: ¥300–¥1,500 for most consultations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Tokyo more expensive than New York or London? In most categories, no. Tokyo rents, food, and transport are generally more affordable than New York or London, especially when you factor in quality and convenience.

Can I live in Tokyo on ¥200,000/month ($1,300)? Yes, comfortably for a single person. You’d have a decent 1K or 1DK apartment, eat well (mix of home cooking and restaurants), and have money left for entertainment.

Do I need to speak Japanese to live in Tokyo? Not necessarily. Many parts of Tokyo are foreigner-friendly, with English signage in stations and many restaurants. However, learning basic Japanese makes daily life much easier.
