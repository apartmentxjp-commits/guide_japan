Japan Tourist Visa Guide: Everything You Need to Know (2025)

January 10, 2026 · 3 min read ·
tourist visa travel japan visa requirements japan entry

Japan is one of the world’s most popular travel destinations — and for good reason. But before you pack your bags, you need to understand Japan’s entry requirements. Here’s everything you need to know about Japan’s tourist visa in 2025.

Do You Need a Visa to Visit Japan?

It depends on your nationality. Japan has visa-exemption agreements with over 60 countries, meaning citizens of those countries can visit without a visa for short stays (typically 15, 30, or 90 days).

Visa-exempt countries include (90-day stay):

  • United States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand
  • Most EU/EEA countries (Germany, France, UK, etc.)
  • Singapore, South Korea, Hong Kong

Countries that require a visa include:

  • India, Pakistan, Bangladesh
  • China (standard passport holders)
  • Most African and Middle Eastern countries

Always check the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan for the latest list — it changes periodically.

How to Apply for a Japan Tourist Visa

If your country requires a visa, here’s the step-by-step process:

  1. Contact your nearest Japanese embassy or consulate — apply in your country of residence, not Japan.
  2. Prepare your documents (see list below).
  3. Submit your application — in person or via authorized travel agent (depending on country).
  4. Wait for processing — typically 5–7 business days.
  5. Collect your visa — a sticker placed in your passport.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond your trip)
  • Completed visa application form (download from embassy website)
  • Recent passport-size photo (white background)
  • Round-trip flight reservation
  • Hotel reservation or invitation letter
  • Bank statement (showing sufficient funds — typically ¥200,000+)
  • Travel itinerary

Some embassies may request additional documents. Always verify with your specific embassy.

Can I Extend My Tourist Visa in Japan?

In most cases, no. Tourist visas and visa-exemption periods are not easily extended. If you overstay, you risk being deported and banned from re-entering Japan.

If you plan to stay longer, consider:

  • A student visa (if enrolling in a Japanese language school)
  • A work visa (if you have a job offer)
  • A long-stay tourism program (some regions offer special programs)

Read our guide on Japan work visa options if you’re considering working in Japan.

Tips for Smooth Entry at Japanese Customs

  1. Complete the arrival card honestly — write your hotel address, not “TBD.”
  2. Have proof of onward travel — immigration may ask for your return flight booking.
  3. Declare everything — Japan has strict customs rules. When in doubt, declare it.
  4. Learn basic Japanese greetings — not required, but appreciated.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long can I stay in Japan without a visa? For citizens of visa-exempt countries (e.g., USA, UK, EU), you can typically stay up to 90 days per visit. Some countries are limited to 15 or 30 days.

Can I work on a Japan tourist visa? No. Working on a tourist visa or visa-exemption status is illegal in Japan. You need a proper work visa.

What happens if I overstay my visa? Overstaying is treated seriously. You may be detained, deported, and banned from re-entering Japan for 1–5 years (or permanently in severe cases).
